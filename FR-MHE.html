Abstract

Accurate and low-latency state estimation is essential for robust quadrotor perception, directly influencing downstream planning and control modules. This paper proposes FR-MHE (Fast and Robust Moving Horizon Estimation), a real-time state estimation framework for quadrotors operating under complex real-world disturbances. We first extend the classical MHE formulation by augmenting the system dynamics with explicit disturbance states (leading to RMHE), modelling three-axis external forces and moments to enhance robustness against unmodeled dynamics and environmental perturbations. To address the high computation typically associated with MHE, particularly introduced by disturbance state augmentation, we then draw a lightweight optimization strategy based on single-iteration gradient descent. Extensive comparative evaluations in both Gazebo simulations and real-world Crazyflie experiments show that FR-MHE achieves a 50 X speed-up in computational efficiency (reducing iteration time from 14.30 ms to 0.29 ms) over RMHE while preserving high estimation accuracy. While compared to the SOTA (Robust) Extended Kalman Filter, FR-MHE achieves superior robustness and precision, making it well-suited for agile, resource-constrained aerial platforms.

Contributions

(1) Disturbance Resilience: we introduce explicit disturbance augmentation in system model, enabling FR-MHE to effectively handle unknown disturbances forces and moments that affect both translational and rotational dynamics of the quadrotor.

(2) Computation Efficiency: we adopt a single-iteration gradient descent in FR-MHE to reduce the computation burden of traditional MHE, especially under increased state dimensions due to disturbance state augmentation. The computational time complexity is only $\mathcal{O}(N \cdot n^3)$.

(3) Experimental validation: we conduct comprehensive evaluations of FR-MHE against SOTA (R)MHE and (R)EKF methods using real-world Crazyflie 2.1 platform and Gazebo simulations, covering various levels of measurement noise and disturbances.

We conduct three experiments to demonstrate the advantages of FR-MHE for quadrotor state estimation in different environments. Fig. (a)–(d) present the results of FR-MHE and FMHE under the Extreme Case with severe disturbances. The results show that FR-MHE consistently exhibits superior generalization, compensates for both internal and external disturbances, and improves state estimation accuracy. Fig. (e) and (f) present the results under different flight trajectories. They demonstrate that although FR-MHE adopts a lightweight computational strategy, it does not sacrifice state estimation effectiveness, and its estimation performance surpasses that of REKF. In the real-world experiments, we introduce wind disturbances of 1.5–4.6 m/s and employ the CHINGMU motion capture system to obtain groundtruth pose data of the Crazyflie UAV. Fig. (a) and (b) demonstrate that the state augmentation scheme enables FR-MHE to maintain strong estimation performance across different noise levels. Fig. (c) shows the computational efficiency of different algorithms. The average computation time for FR-MHE are 0.29 ms (N=3) and 0.36 ms (N=4), representing a 50–70 X speedup compared with RMHE. Fig. (d), (f), and (g) present multi-trajectory comparisons across different algorithms, while Fig. (e) shows the estimation performance under non-Gaussian (heavy-tailed) noise. It demonstrates that FR-MHE maintains an error profile that remains close to Gaussian, with only marginal degradation in accuracy. This highlights its strong robustness to outliers and heavy-tailed noise, achieving performance comparable to full RMHE.
